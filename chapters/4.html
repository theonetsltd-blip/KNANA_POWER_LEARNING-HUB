Key Concepts

Ergonomics

Study of workplace design to prevent injury and discomfort

Posture

The way you position your body while sitting and working

RSI (Repetitive Strain Injury)

Pain and injury from repeating the same movement (e.g., typing)

Cable Management

Organizing cables to avoid tangling, tripping, or damage

4.1 Proper Desk & Chair Setup

üí∫ Chair Setup

  • Height: Feet flat on floor, knees at 90¬∞ angle
  • Back support: Back firmly against chair back
  • Armrests: Elbows resting at 90¬∞
  • Lumbar support: Lower back should have support

üìè Desk Height

  • Distance: 50-60 cm from floor (about elbow height when arms bent)
  • Monitor at eye level: Top of screen at or slightly below eye level
  • Distance from monitor: About 50-60 cm (arm's length)

‚å®Ô∏è Keyboard & Mouse Placement

  • Keyboard and mouse same height as elbow
  • Keep mouse close to keyboard
  • Wrists straight, not bent down

üí° Remember:

The monitor should be at EYE LEVEL. Your neck should not bend up or down. This prevents neck strain.

4.2 Cable Management & Connections

üìã Power Setup:

  1. Use a power strip/surge protector (NOT regular extension cord)
  2. Connect surge protector to wall outlet
  3. Plug in: monitor, keyboard, mouse, printer, etc.
  4. Never overload (max 1500W for standard outlet)
  5. Keep cables away from water and heat

üîå Cable Organization:

‚úÖ Do:

  • Secure cables with clips
  • Keep cables behind desk
  • Group by purpose
  • Label cables

‚ùå Don't:

  • Stretch cables across walkway
  • Leave loose coils
  • Place heavy objects on cables
  • Wrap tightly around objects

4.3 Health Risks & Prevention

üëÅÔ∏è Eye Strain

Problem: Blurred vision, headaches, dry eyes

Prevention: 20-20-20 rule: Every 20 mins, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds

ü§ï Repetitive Strain Injury (RSI)

Problem: Pain in wrists, fingers, shoulders from repetitive typing/clicking

Prevention: Keep wrists straight, take breaks, stretch regularly

ü™¨ Back & Neck Pain

Problem: Slouching, monitor too high/low, poor posture

Prevention: Keep monitor at eye level, sit upright, use chair with lumbar support

ü¶µ Poor Circulation

Problem: Sitting too long without movement

Prevention: Stand and stretch every 30-45 minutes, walk around, do light exercises

4.4 Workspace Cleanliness & Organization

üßπ Keeping Your Workspace Clean:

  • Daily: Wipe down desk and keyboard with soft cloth
  • Weekly: Clean monitor screen with microfiber cloth
  • Monthly: Dust inside (tower) with compressed air
  • Never: Spill liquid on computer equipment

üì¶ Organization Tips:

‚òëÔ∏è Do:

  • Keep desk free of clutter
  • Store cables in cable box
  • Place only needed items within reach
  • Have document holder beside monitor

‚ùå Don't:

  • Pile papers and books randomly
  • Leave cups/bottles near equipment
  • Block ventilation vents
  • Cover cables with cloth

Chapter Summary

  • Proper ergonomics prevents injury and discomfort during computer work
  • Monitor at eye level prevents neck strain
  • Organized cables prevent tripping hazards and equipment damage
  • Regular breaks reduce health risks like RSI and eye strain
  • Clean workspace keeps equipment working longer
